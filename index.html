The Grand Slams

The four major championships, known as Grand Slams, are the most prestigious tournaments in tennis. They offer the most ranking points, prize money, and public attention. Winning all four in a single year is the ultimate achievement.

The Happy Slam

Melbourne, AUS Hard Court January

The first major of the year, played in the peak of the Australian summer. It is known for its extreme heat, testing the physical endurance of players right at the start of the season. Played on blue Plexicushion hard courts.

The French Open

Paris, FRA Red Clay May - June

The most physically demanding tournament in the world. The crushed brick (clay) surface slows down the ball and produces a high bounce, leading to long, grueling rallies. It strongly favors defensive baseline players.

The Championships

London, UK Grass Court July

The oldest and arguably most prestigious tennis tournament in the world. Characterized by its strict all-white dress code for competitors, Royal patronage, and strawberries and cream. The grass surface is fast and favors serve-and-volley play and slice.
